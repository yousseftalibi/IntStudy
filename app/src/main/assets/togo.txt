Here are some short details about Togo based on your request:
Accommodation: You can find different types of accommodation in Togo, from hotels and guesthouses to apartments and homestays. The average price for a hotel room is about 30,000 CFA francs (45 euros) per night, while a furnished apartment can cost between 100,000 and 300,000 CFA francs (150 to 450 euros) per month. You can use websites like [Booking.com](^1^), [Airbnb](^2^) or [Jumia Travel](^3^) to search and book accommodation online.
Cost of living: The cost of living in Togo is relatively low compared to France. The local currency is the West African CFA franc (XOF), which is pegged to the euro at a fixed rate of 655.957 XOF for 1 euro. You can exchange or withdraw euros at banks, ATMs or bureaux de change. A monthly budget of 400,000 CFA francs (600 euros) should be enough to cover your basic expenses such as food, transport, utilities and entertainment.
Transport: Togo has a cheap public transport network that consists of buses, taxis and motorcycle taxis. You can also rent a car or a bike if you prefer more flexibility. To travel within the country, you can use buses, trains or planes, depending on the distance and your budget. You can use websites like [Rome2rio](^4^) or [Skyscanner](^5^) to compare prices and options.
Climate and seasons: Togo has a tropical climate that varies depending on the region and the altitude. The south has two rainy seasons, from March to June and from September to October, while the north has one rainy season from May to October. The dry season lasts from November to February in the south and from November to April in the north. The average temperature ranges from 23°C to 32°C throughout the year. The seasons are reversed compared to France, so when it is summer in France, it is winter in Togo.
Culture and leisure: Togo has a rich and diverse culture that reflects its 37 ethnic groups and their traditions. Some of the cultural highlights include the voodoo religion, the Ewe statuettes, the Kabye pottery, the batik fabrics and the chains of marriage. Togo also has a varied and tasty gastronomy that features dishes such as fufu, koklo meme, ablo and wagasi. The people of Togo are warm and festive, and they enjoy music and dance such as tango, highlife and hip hop. The nightlife in Togo is lively and offers many options such as cinema, theater, museum, café, bar or nightclub.
Tourism and nature: Togo is a great destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The country offers varied and spectacular landscapes such as lakes, waterfalls, forests, mountains, savannahs and beaches. You can enjoy activities such as trekking, biking, rafting, surfing, fishing or wildlife observation. Some of the unmissable sites in Togo are the Koutammakou cultural landscape (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), the Fazao-Malfakassa National Park (the largest park in West Africa), the Cascade de Kpime (a stunning waterfall), the Lac Togo (a scenic lake), the Palais de Lomé (a historic palace) and the Grand Marché (a colorful market). You can find guides and blogs for traveling to Togo on websites like [Lonely Planet](^6^) or [Exploring Africa](^7^).
